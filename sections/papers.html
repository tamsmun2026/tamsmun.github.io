Submitting Your Position Paper

01

Research Your Country & Topic

Read the background guide for your committee. Research your assigned country's position on the topic using government sources, UN documents, and reputable news outlets.

02

Write Your Paper

Follow the format outlined below. Position papers should be 1–2 pages single-spaced and submitted as a PDF. Clearly state your country's stance and proposed solutions.

03

Submit via the Portal

Upload your PDF through the delegate submission portal (link sent after registration). Include your name, school, committee, and assigned country in the filename.

04

Deadline: Sept 10, 2026

Submissions due by 11:59 PM CST. Papers submitted on time are eligible for the Best Position Paper award. Late papers may still be submitted but will not be judged.

Position Paper Requirements

  • 1–2 pages, single spaced, 12pt font
  • Times New Roman or similar serif font
  • 1-inch margins on all sides
  • Submitted as a PDF file
  • Filename: LastName_Country_Committee.pdf
  • Introduction: country background & stance
  • Body: analysis of the topic and current UN efforts
  • Conclusion: proposed solutions or resolutions
  • Bibliography required (not counted in page limit)
  • No plagiarism — submissions are checked
