Position Papers

Submitting Your Position Paper

01

Research Your Country & Topic

Read the background guide for your committee. Research your assigned country's position on the topic using government sources, UN documents, and reputable news outlets.

02

Write Your Paper

Follow the format outlined below. Position papers should be 1–2 pages single-spaced and submitted as a PDF. Clearly state your country's stance and proposed solutions.

03

Submit via the Portal

Upload your PDF through the delegate submission portal (link sent after registration). Include your name, school, committee, and assigned country in the filename.

04

Deadline: Sept 10, 2026

Submissions due by 11:59 PM CST. Papers submitted on time are eligible for the Best Position Paper award. Late papers may still be submitted but will not be judged.

Position Paper Requirements

  • 1–2 pages, single spaced, 12pt font
  • Times New Roman or similar serif font
  • 1-inch margins on all sides
  • Submitted as a PDF file
  • Filename: LastName_Country_Committee.pdf
  • Introduction: country background & stance
  • Body: analysis of the topic and current UN efforts
  • Conclusion: proposed solutions or resolutions
  • Bibliography required (not counted in page limit)
  • No plagiarism — submissions are checked
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

TAMS MUN 2026 is open to all high school students (grades 9–12). Schools from across Texas and neighboring states are welcome to register. Delegates must be enrolled in a high school at the time of the conference.

After your school's initial registration is confirmed, your faculty advisor will receive a link to a delegate roster form. Within that form, you can indicate country and committee preferences for each delegate. Country assignments are released Sept 1, 2026, and are made on a first-come, first-served basis based on registration date.

TAMS MUN does not provide on-campus housing. However, there are several hotels within a 10-minute drive of the UNT campus in Denton. We will share a list of recommended hotels with a conference room block code in your confirmation email. Schools are responsible for arranging their own accommodations and transportation.

Western business attire is required for all committee sessions. This means collared shirts, dress pants or skirts, blazers, and dress shoes. Jeans, sneakers, and casual clothing are not permitted in committee rooms. Business casual is acceptable for the opening social and dinner event on Friday evening.

Background guides for all committees will be published on this website and emailed to registered delegates on September 1, 2026, the same day as country assignment releases. Each guide contains an overview of the committee topic, key terms, bloc positions, and guiding questions to help delegates prepare.

Awards are determined by committee chairs based on delegates' participation, preparation, diplomacy, and resolution-writing. Categories include Best Delegate, Outstanding Delegate, Best Position Paper, and Best Large Delegation. Awards are presented at the closing ceremony on the final day of the conference.
