About the Conference

Diplomacy Starts Here

TAMS MUN 2026 is hosted by the Texas Academy of Mathematics and Science at the University of North Texas in Denton, Texas. This conference brings together high school students from across the state for two days of rigorous debate, coalition-building, and international policy-making.

Whether you're a first-time delegate or a seasoned MUN veteran, TAMS MUN 2026 offers a challenging and rewarding environment to develop your public speaking, research, and critical thinking skills.

Our experienced secretariat and faculty advisors are committed to running an inclusive, educational, and professionally organized conference that reflects the standards of real United Nations proceedings.

University Setting

Experience conference sessions held in the university facilities of UNT's Denton campus — a genuine collegiate environment for high school delegates.

All Experience Levels

Committees are structured for beginner, intermediate, and advanced delegates. Every school and every experience level is welcome.

Awards & Recognition

Outstanding delegates, best position papers, and best delegations will be recognized at the closing ceremony on the final day.

Hosted by TAMS Students

Organized entirely by current TAMS students — a student-run conference bringing peer-to-peer learning in international affairs.

Position Papers

Submitting Your Position Paper

01

Research Your Country & Topic

Read the background guide for your committee (released Nov 15). Research your assigned country's position on the topic using government sources, UN documents, and reputable news outlets.

02

Write Your Paper

Follow the format outlined below. Position papers should be 1–2 pages single-spaced and submitted as a PDF. Clearly state your country's stance and proposed solutions.

03

Submit via the Portal

Upload your PDF through the delegate submission portal (link sent after registration). Include your name, school, committee, and assigned country in the filename.

04

Deadline: January 10, 2026

Submissions due by 11:59 PM CST. Papers submitted on time are eligible for the Best Position Paper award. Late papers may still be submitted but will not be judged.

Position Paper Requirements

  • 1–2 pages, single spaced, 12pt font
  • Times New Roman or similar serif font
  • 1-inch margins on all sides
  • Submitted as a PDF file
  • Filename: LastName_Country_Committee.pdf
  • Introduction: country background & stance
  • Body: analysis of the topic and current UN efforts
  • Conclusion: proposed solutions or resolutions
  • Bibliography required (not counted in page limit)
  • No plagiarism — submissions are checked
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

TAMS MUN 2026 is open to all high school students (grades 9–12). Schools from across Texas and neighboring states are welcome to register. Delegates must be enrolled in a high school at the time of the conference.

After your school's initial registration is confirmed, your faculty advisor will receive a link to a delegate roster form. Within that form, you can indicate country and committee preferences for each delegate. Country assignments are released on November 15, 2025, and are made on a first-come, first-served basis based on registration date.

TAMS MUN does not provide on-campus housing. However, there are several hotels within a 10-minute drive of the UNT campus in Denton. We will share a list of recommended hotels with a conference room block code in your confirmation email. Schools are responsible for arranging their own accommodations and transportation.

Not at all! We offer beginner-friendly committees like GA1 and GA3 specifically designed for first-time delegates. Background guides and a pre-conference delegate training webinar (held in January) are provided to help all delegates prepare regardless of experience level.

Western business attire is required for all committee sessions. This means collared shirts, dress pants or skirts, blazers, and dress shoes. Jeans, sneakers, and casual clothing are not permitted in committee rooms. Business casual is acceptable for the opening social and dinner event on Friday evening.

Background guides for all committees will be published on this website and emailed to registered delegates on November 15, 2025, the same day as country assignment releases. Each guide contains an overview of the committee topic, key terms, bloc positions, and guiding questions to help delegates prepare.

Awards are determined by committee chairs based on delegates' participation, preparation, diplomacy, and resolution-writing. Categories include Best Delegate, Outstanding Delegate, Best Position Paper, and Best Large Delegation. Awards are presented at the closing ceremony on the final day of the conference.
